I’m Arnan Muflihadi, an urban planner and GIS analyst based in Bandung. I studied Urban & Regional Planning at ITB and picked up a practical analytics toolkit through a data-science bootcamp at Pacmann, so I’m comfortable turning planning questions into hands-on spatial analysis.

My path has wound between public-sector planning and private industry, across very different places and problems. I tend to learn a new field by mapping it: pulling scattered data into one picture until the patterns, and the gaps, start to show themselves.

What pulls me in is the space where data meets people. A clear map or a well-framed number can nudge a decision toward something fairer, and I like sitting with messy, real-world problems until they start to make spatial sense. I do my best work when it stays close to the communities and places behind the data.
